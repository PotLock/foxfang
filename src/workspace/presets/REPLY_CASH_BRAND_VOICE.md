# Reply Cash — Brand Voice

## Voice Goal
Make cross-border stablecoin payouts feel clear, credible, and immediately usable.

## Voice Attributes
- Clear
- Practical
- Fast-moving but not hypey
- Trust-first
- Globally aware, Africa-execution focused

## Primary Tone
Straightforward and outcome-driven.
Lead with user value, then show how it works.

## Secondary Tone
Confident and optimistic when announcing launches or new corridors.
Still avoid exaggerated certainty.

## Tone Rules
- Use short sentences and concrete verbs.
- Prefer "send", "receive", "convert", "payout", "bridge".
- Explain local rails by name when relevant (for example M-Pesa, OPay, MTN).
- Keep technical words only when they improve clarity.

## Vocabulary Preferences
Prefer:
- "send stablecoins locally"
- "mobile money payout"
- "bank payout"
- "where live"
- "fees typically under ~2% (where live)"
- "often under ~60s to mobile money (where live)"

Avoid:
- "guaranteed"
- "risk-free"
- "instant everywhere"
- "moonshot"
- speculative trading slang

## Messaging Structure
Use this order for most content:
1. User outcome:
What the sender and recipient achieve.
2. Mechanism:
Stablecoin in, local rail out.
3. Scope:
Which corridor/rail is live.
4. CTA:
Try flow, check countries/routes, or start in app.

## Claim Safety Guardrails
- Always scope speed/fee claims to corridor conditions.
- Do not universalize live status across all countries.
- If unsure, suggest checking current `countries` or route pages.

## Channel-Specific Style
- Landing/blog:
Educational, confidence-building, less shorthand.
- Social:
Crisp, punchy, outcome-first.
- Product help copy:
Step-by-step, plain language, no fluff.

## CTA Style
Preferred:
- "Check live corridors"
- "See supported routes"
- "Start a payout"
- "Bridge and send"

Avoid:
- pressure-based urgency ("last chance", "act now or lose")

## Quality Check Before Publish
- Is the claim specific to a rail/country when needed?
- Can a user understand the action in 5 seconds?
- Is the copy credible without overclaiming?
