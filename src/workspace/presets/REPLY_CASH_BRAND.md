# Reply Cash — Brand

## Official Product Identity
- Product: `reply.cash`
- Primary site (marketing): `https://reply.cash/`
- App: `https://app.reply.cash`
- Docs: `https://docs.reply.cash`
- Core promise (homepage): send stablecoins to local rails where live.

## Brand Positioning
Reply Cash positions itself as a practical cross-border payments product:
- Send stablecoins (mainly USDC/USDT).
- Payout to local payment rails (mobile money + bank accounts).
- Recipient experience focuses on local currency receipt and low friction.

## What Reply Cash Is About
- Stablecoin -> local money conversion for real-world payouts.
- Mobile-money-first and bank-friendly rails in key markets.
- Cross-chain movement (bridge) before payout.
- Fast, transparent, and utility-first money movement.

## Key Message Pillars
1. Local payout utility:
Recipients get money through rails they already use.

2. Speed and convenience:
Marketing emphasizes fast transfers and simple flow.

3. Cost clarity:
Marketing highlights low fees (often framed as under ~2% where live).

4. Chain flexibility:
Promotes support for many chains and bridge workflows.

## Market Focus (from site messaging)
- Live emphasis: Kenya, Nigeria, Uganda.
- Broader expansion narrative: additional African corridors and selected other regions.
- Country/route status is dynamic; use on-site status as source of truth.

## Product Claims Discipline
When creating content for Reply Cash:
- Keep claims tied to "where live" corridors.
- Do not present marketing numbers as universal guarantees.
- Avoid absolute speed/fee promises in all cases.
- Prefer precise wording over hype.

## Allowed Proof Points (website-level)
- Stablecoin to local rails (mobile money + bank).
- Recipient may not need a crypto wallet for payout experience.
- Bridge and multi-chain support are part of product narrative.
- Africa corridors are core GTM focus.

## Not Allowed in Messaging
- "Guaranteed profit", "instant riches", or speculative framing.
- Misleading "works everywhere right now" statements.
- Any claim that conflicts with corridor-live status on current pages.
- Any legal/compliance promises not present in official docs.

## Content Priorities
- Explain user outcome first: who receives what, how, and where.
- Keep copy action-oriented and concrete.
- Make operational scope explicit: corridor, rail, asset, expected flow.
